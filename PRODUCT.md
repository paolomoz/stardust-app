# Product

## Register

brand

> The design IS the product — stardust is a crafted creative instrument, and
> using it should feel like holding one. But it is also a working tool, so
> every surface stays functional, legible, and fast. Brand stance, product
> discipline.

## Users

Designers and engineers who build and ship websites — agency/presales teams
pitching a redesign, and in-house AEM teams modernizing a live site. They are
sophisticated and taste-driven, fluent in the difference between distinctive
work and generic LLM output, and impatient with fluff. They arrive with a URL
and a hunch ("this site could be so much better") and want to get to something
beautiful fast — then, when convinced, take it all the way to production on
Adobe Experience Manager.

Their context: a focused work session, often a screen-share or a pitch in the
background. They are the **director**; stardust is the crew. They want to make
a handful of decisions that matter and have the craft handled.

## Product Purpose

stardust redesigns existing websites and ships them to AEM, through a
**gradual-complexity ladder** where every rung is independently valuable:

- **Explore** — paste a URL, get three redesign directions (one cinematic),
  pick one and refine it by conversation.
- **Templates** — turn the chosen direction into a few reusable page-type
  templates.
- **Deploy** — push those templates to AEM Edge Delivery as blocks + content.
- **Rollout** — apply the templates across the rest of the site, at the
  user's pace.

Most users start at Explore and many stop there with a direction they love.
Success is: a user pastes a URL and, within minutes, is looking at a redesign
that is unmistakably *theirs* and unmistakably *not* generic — and can climb
toward production whenever they're ready, never forced to.

The interaction is **conversational-first**: an agent conversation is the
spine of every screen, and rich visual panels (palette picker, three-up
variant gallery, live prototype preview, page grid, deploy status) render
inline within it.

## Brand Personality

A master design crafter with **strong opinions quietly held**. It knows it
produces great work and never says so — it just does it, and keeps things
very simple for the user. A quiet pro instrument with **recognizable
signature moments** placed deliberately, not everywhere.

Three words: **quiet · exact · confident.**

Voice (five rules, carried verbatim from the stardust identity):
1. **Call it the thing.** "Palette," not "chromatic symphony." Plain words.
2. **Show the seed.** Everything generated surfaces its provenance.
   Transparency is a feature.
3. **Math, not mysticism.** "Hashed" and "seeded," never "inspired" or
   "divined."
4. **Honor the designer.** You direct; stardust is the crew. Never claim the
   tool made the design.
5. **Short is a virtue.** Clipped sentences. No throat-clearing. Cut any word
   not earning its keep.

Emotional goal: the calm confidence of a precise instrument in capable hands —
never hype, never the breathless AI-product register.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS gradients** — cyan→purple, the LLM-default hero. Banned.
- **Glassmorphism** as decoration. Blur is rare and purposeful or absent.
- **Dark-mode-by-reflex clichés** — neon-on-charcoal, "developer cool."
  stardust is dark because Ink is its ground, not to look edgy.
- **Mystical / over-promising AI copy** — "unleash limitless creative
  possibility," "let the magic of inspiration…" The identity's own Don't column.
- **Feature-dense dashboards** — walls of panels, toolbars, and controls.
  stardust is dense-and-*simple*, never dense-and-*featured*.
- **Anything shouty** — heavy weights, all-caps prose, exclamation, urgency
  theater. Confidence is quiet.

## Design Principles

1. **Simplicity is the feature.** One clear thing to do per moment. Hide the
   machinery; surface the decision. If a control isn't earning the screen,
   cut it.
2. **The conversation is the spine.** Lead with dialogue and reasoning;
   visual panels serve the conversation, never compete with it.
3. **Show the seed.** Every generated artifact carries visible provenance —
   the work is reproducible, and we prove it rather than mystify it.
4. **Restraint is the brand.** Five colors, three type voices, one accent per
   view. Signatures land *because* the surrounding surface is quiet.
5. **Honor the director.** The user makes the decisions that matter; stardust
   does the craft and gets out of the way.
6. **Every rung pays off.** No screen is merely a step toward a later reward;
   each stage of the ladder delivers something real on its own.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 against its ground; large text
  ≥ 3:1. On Ink `#0A1024`, body uses Dust at ≥ 0.72 opacity (verified), not
  faint grays. Amber on Ink and Ink on Amber both pass; never amber text on
  amber.
- **Amber is a signal, not a sole carrier of meaning.** State is always
  reinforced with text/label or shape, so amber/color-blind users aren't lost.
- **Reduced motion is honored.** The dust field, radial grounds, and any
  reveal have a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` path (crossfade or static).
  Motion is restrained by default.
- **Keyboard-complete.** Conversation, pickers, variant selection, and
  approval are all reachable and operable without a pointer; visible focus
  rings in amber.
- **Mono is for machines, not prose.** Seeds, commands, and provenance use
  mono; reading copy never does — protecting legibility for everyone.
